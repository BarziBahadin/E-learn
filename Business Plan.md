# E-Lern Business Plan

## 1. Business Summary

E-Lern is a paid video-learning platform for Grade 12 students in the Kurdistan Region of Iraq. It provides curriculum-focused courses from trusted local teachers through web, Android, and iOS applications.

## 2. Problem

Students often struggle to find:

- High-quality lessons matching the local curriculum
- Reliable teachers outside major cities
- Structured exam preparation
- Affordable alternatives to private tutoring
- Secure access to purchased digital courses

## 3. Solution

E-Lern allows students to:

- Browse subjects, teachers, and courses
- Watch free preview lessons
- Purchase courses using local payment methods
- Resume lessons from their last position
- Access PDFs, quizzes, and exam preparation
- Receive learning reminders and support

## 4. Target Market

Primary customers:

- Grade 12 students in the Kurdistan Region
- Parents purchasing courses for their children

Future markets:

- Grades 9–11
- University preparation
- Professional and language courses

## 5. Revenue Model

- Individual course purchases
- Subject bundles
- Full-year subscriptions
- School licensing
- Premium exam-revision packages

Example pricing:

| Product | Suggested Price |
|---|---:|
| Individual course | 40,000–60,000 IQD |
| Subject bundle | 80,000–120,000 IQD |
| Annual subscription | 250,000–350,000 IQD |

## 6. Teacher Partnership

Teachers receive an agreed percentage of course revenue. E-Lern provides:

- Video hosting and protection
- Payment collection
- Marketing and student acquisition
- Course analytics
- Technical and customer support

## 7. Marketing Strategy

- Teacher social-media audiences
- Facebook, Instagram, TikTok, and YouTube
- Free preview lessons
- Student referral discounts
- School and tutoring-center partnerships
- Exam-season promotional campaigns

## 8. Operations

The initial team should include:

- Founder or product manager
- Mobile/web developer
- Content and teacher coordinator
- Video editor
- Customer-support representative
- Digital marketer

## 9. Key Costs

- Software development and maintenance
- Video hosting and bandwidth
- Teacher revenue sharing
- Video production and editing
- Marketing
- Payment-processing fees
- Customer support

## 10. First-Year Goals

- Recruit 5–10 respected teachers
- Publish courses for the main Grade 12 subjects
- Reach 2,000 registered students
- Convert at least 10% into paying customers
- Maintain strong course completion and customer satisfaction

## 11. Main Risks

- Video piracy and account sharing
- Inconsistent teacher content quality
- Limited local online-payment adoption
- High video bandwidth costs
- Seasonal demand around examination periods

## 12. Launch Plan

1. Launch Physics, Mathematics, and Chemistry courses.
2. Partner with three recognized teachers.
3. Test the platform with 100–200 students.
4. Improve content and payments using feedback.
5. Run a larger exam-season marketing campaign.
6. Expand into additional subjects and grades.
